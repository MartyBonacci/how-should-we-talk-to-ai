# Research & Resources

A collection of research, articles, and resources related to AI communication, sycophancy, and human-AI collaboration.

## Academic Research

### Anthropic

- **[Towards Understanding Sycophancy in Language Models](https://www.anthropic.com/research/towards-understanding-sycophancy-in-language-models)** — Research showing that AI assistants trained with RLHF tend to produce responses that match what users want to hear rather than what's accurate.

- **[Sycophancy to Subterfuge: Exploring Reward Tampering](https://www.anthropic.com/research/reward-tampering)** — How simple approval-seeking behavior can generalize to more sophisticated behaviors. Demonstrates that training away sycophancy reduces other problematic behaviors.

- **[Claude's Character](https://www.anthropic.com/research/claude-character)** — How Anthropic approaches character training, including the goal of making AI models genuinely honest rather than just pleasingly confident.

- **[How People Use Claude for Support, Advice, and Companionship](https://www.anthropic.com/news/how-people-use-claude-for-support-advice-and-companionship)** — Research on affective conversations with AI, including findings that people shift toward more positive emotional expressions while talking to Claude.
